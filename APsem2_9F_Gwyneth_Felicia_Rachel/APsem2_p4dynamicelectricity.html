    Voltmeter

    The voltmeter is a measurement device used to measure voltage in electrical circuits or electronic devices. It is commonly employed to measure the voltage across a circuit or component, typically expressed in volts.

    Ammeter

    An ammeter is a measuring instrument used in electrical circuits. Its primary purpose is to measure electric current and express the intensity of the current. Electric current represents the speed of charged particles passing through a circuit and is typically measured in amperes (A).

    Electric Field

  • The direction of the electric field moves from positive to negative
  • The direction of electron movement moves from negative to positive.
  • Positive charge will move with the electric field
  • Negative charge will move against the electric field

    Kirchoff's Law

    Kirchoff's Current Law (KCL)

    The total current entering the junction equals to the total current leaving the junction.

    Kirchoff's Voltage Law (KVL)

    The total electromotive force (emf) in a closed circuit is equal to the sum of the potential differences in the circuit
