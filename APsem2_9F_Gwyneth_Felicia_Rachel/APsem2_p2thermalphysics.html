THERMAL PHYSICS


Definition:

Thermal Physics is the relationship between heat, temperature, and energy, and their interactions with matter.


States Of Matter

There are three states of matter:


Particle Movement

Particles can move faster or slower depending on the temperature.

When an object is heated, it gains an increase in its internal energy. The higher the temperature, the higher the average internal energy. With more energy, molecules will vibrate faster. In solids, molecules vibrate faster and take up more space, which causes expansion.


Specific Heat Capacity

The specific heat capacity of an object or substance is the amount of energy needed to heat 1 kilogram of a substance by 1°C.

Specific Latent Heat

Specific Latent Heat or SLH is the amount of energy required to change 1kg of one substance from a particular state to another.

Specific Latent Heat of Fusion
The specific latent heat of fusion is one of 2 types of specific latent heat. It is the energy change when a substance changes between a solid or liquid. (Melting or freezing)

Specific Latent Heat of Vaporisation
The specific latent heat of vaporisation is the 2nd type of specific latent heat. It is the energy change when a substance changes between a liquid and a gas. (vaporisation or condensation)
